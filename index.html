Hardware or Software?
Why not Both?

Technology has evolved so much over the decade and the field is expanding so rapidly that one could easily immerse themselves in one facet of the indstury without ever having to learn about the other .

While there may be nothing inheriently wrong with this approach, I can't help but think that in order to be a true driver of innovation, its imperative to have an understanding of both sides of the industry.

That's why I've chosen to combine my developing skills in software development with my curiousity for hardware. Currently, I'm accomplishing this by experimenting with the Raspberry Pi, a linux based computer that fits into the palm of my hand. Learning how to use a Pi is also fueling my desire to learn Python, as it is the primary language that I've been using to create my Pi projects.
